Todd Combs departed Berkshire late 2025 to run JPMorgan Chase's banking operations. Abel used his first quarter to systematically exit positions he didn't personally underwrite — including Visa and Mastercard (held since 2011), which represented the most symbolically significant departures in modern Berkshire history.

What This Signals
Reading between the lines of Abel's debut
🔺
Alphabet is the AI bargain
Abel tripled GOOGL while peers like Ackman and Druckenmiller cut. Combined with a US$10B private placement into Alphabet's AI build, Abel sees Google as the cheapest unimpeachable AI moat in mega-cap tech — cheaper than Amazon or Microsoft at current valuations.
🏦
Cash is the loudest signal
US$397B in T-bills earning ~5% is not laziness — it's a position. Berkshire is signalling that US equities broadly don't offer sufficient margin of safety at current prices. The implication: be patient, hold dry powder, wait for the pitch.
✈️
Airlines are worth revisiting
Delta is the most symbolically loaded move — directly reversing Buffett's 2020 pledge to never own an airline again. Delta's premium-cabin mix, pricing discipline and industry consolidation have structurally changed the economics since COVID.
🇯🇵
Japan is Abel's first big call
Off the 13F but equally important: Berkshire crossed 10% in all five Japanese trading houses (Itochu, Mitsubishi, Mitsui, Sumitomo, Marubeni) in May 2026. These are cheap, cash-generative conglomerates leveraged to commodities — Buffett's logic applied to an undervalued market.
⚡
Energy: trim after the rally
Cutting Chevron by 35% after its ~40% run is pure valuation discipline. Occidental (OXY) was untouched — Berkshire still believes in the long-term oil thesis, just not at stretched prices. If you're heavy energy, ask: have you rebalanced?
🏠
Housing dip = opportunity
Lennar +43% and small NYT triple, plus Abel's first acquisition being a homebuilder, suggests a contrarian bet on US housing affordability eventually improving. Structural supply shortage isn't going away.
